Run Machine of India

Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most prominent and accomplished cricketers of his generation. Widely regarded as one of the finest batsmen in the world, Kohli has consistently showcased his extraordinary skills and passion for the game. His cricketing journey began at a young age, and he quickly rose through the ranks, making his debut for the Indian national team in August 2008 during an ODI against Sri Lanka. Kohli's meteoric rise in international cricket was marked by his exceptional batting prowess, remarkable consistency, and an unwavering commitment to excellence.

Achievements:

  • Holds numerous records in international cricket, including being the fastest to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs).
  • First player to score 20,000 international runs in a single decade (2010s).
  • Recipient of the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for the ICC Cricketer of the Year on multiple occasions.
  • Awarded the Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting honor, in 2018.
  • Named Wisden Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018.
